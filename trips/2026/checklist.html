📄 Documents & Bookings
Print or save offline copies of everything — Japanese rural areas and airports sometimes have poor data signals.
🔌 Tech & Power
Adapters: Japan uses Type A plugs — same as the US, no adapter needed. UK uses Type G (3-pin). Bring at least 2 UK adapters or one universal.
👕 Clothing — Japan
Tokyo & Osaka: 30–35°C, extremely humid in July — rainy season just ending. Sweat is unavoidable; moisture-wicking fabrics make a real difference. Bring a light layer for heavily air-conditioned interiors (trains, restaurants, convenience stores are kept very cold). Tohoku (Zao/Ginzan): Mountain elevation brings temperatures down to 15–22°C — a light fleece or jacket is needed, especially for the Zao crater hike and evening onsen walks.
🧥 Clothing — London
London in July: Mild and variable — 18–25°C but evenings can drop to 14°C. Rain is always possible regardless of forecast. The key word is layers. You'll go from sunny afternoons to cool breezy evenings within the same day.
👟 Shoes
Japan shoe rule: You will remove your shoes frequently — at ryokan, some restaurants, and some temples. Avoid shoes with complicated laces or hard-to-remove buckles. Slip-ons or easy pull-on shoes save you time and embarrassment at least once a day. You'll also walk 15,000–20,000 steps on busy Tokyo days.
🧴 Health & Toiletries
Japan has excellent pharmacy chains (Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Sundrug) everywhere if you forget anything. Many toiletries are cheaper and higher quality in Japan — sunscreen and skincare especially.

📱 Data & eSIMs
Two eSIMs per person · No pocket WiFi needed

Two eSIMs each, not a shared pocket WiFi. Since Trish runs solo every morning and you'll regularly split up during the day, independent connectivity is essential. eSIM is also significantly cheaper — roughly $90 total for both of you across both countries, versus $200+ for pocket WiFi rentals or carrier day passes.
