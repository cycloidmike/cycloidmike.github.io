Wed · Jul
Henn na Sendai
Sendai Base Day & Matsushima
🏃
Trish: Morning run — Hirose River path, flat, tree-lined, popular with locals. 5–10km along the riverbank through the city.
🌊
Matsushima day trip (~30 min by Senseki Line) — one of Japan's Three Views: pine-covered islands scattered across a bay. Walk coastal paths, visit Zuiganji Temple. Map →
🦪
Grilled oysters at Matsushima — the bay is famous for them, eaten straight from the shell at waterfront stalls. Don't miss.
⛩️
Zuihoden Mausoleum — the ornate resting place of Date Masamune, Sendai's legendary feudal lord, set in a cedar forest above the city. Most visitors skip it entirely; don't be one of them. The lacquered black-and-gold architecture is extraordinary and the cedar path up to it is one of the most atmospheric walks in Tohoku. About 15 min by taxi from Sendai station.
🥩
Gyutan dinner at Rikyu — grilled beef tongue is the signature dish of Sendai and nothing like what you'd find anywhere else. Rikyu is the most famous, with thick-cut tongue served over barley rice with oxtail soup. Queue at dinner or book ahead. This is the must-eat of Tohoku.

→ Zao Kokusai
Naruko Gorge → Zao Onsen
🏞️
Naruko Gorge stop — dramatic forested canyon en route to Yamagata. Easy walking trail, stunning summer green. About 1–2 hours.
🚌
Continue to Zao Onsen — mountain onsen village at ~880m elevation. Noticeably cooler than Tokyo (~22°C in July).
♨️
First onsen evening — kashikiri private bath — the main public baths at Zao (including the famous Dai-rotenburo) are gender-segregated, so book a kashikiri (private rental bath) at your ryokan when you check in. You get a private outdoor bath reserved just for the two of you in a 45–60 min slot — milky sulfuric water, mountain views, completely private. Request a dusk slot. Expect ¥2,000–5,000 extra. Also: the ashiyu (foot baths) on the village streets are always free and mixed — a lovely way to soak together outdoors.
🍜
Dinner at a ryokan or local izakaya — Yamagata beef and local mountain vegetables are the regional speciality

→ Ginzan Ryokan
Ginzan Onsen — The Honeymoon Night 💍
🚌
Transfer to Ginzan Onsen — ~1.5 hours from Zao area via Oishida or Yamagata station. Buses are infrequent — plan timings carefully on Hyperdia.
🏃
Trish: Afternoon run — the Ginzan River valley road, flat and scenic, run out along the stream and back into the hills
🏮
Dusk at Ginzan — gas lamps light up as the sun sets. Three-storey Taisho-era wooden ryokan reflected in the river. This is the Spirited Away moment. Don't miss it.
♨️
Ryokan onsen & kaiseki dinner — stay at Notoya Ryokan or Fujiya. When booking, request a kashikiri (private rental) bath slot — both ryokan offer these, and a private outdoor bath at Ginzan with gas lamps reflecting on the river at night is one of the most memorable things you can do in Japan. Book the 9–10pm slot after dinner. Multi-course kaiseki dinner included with the room.
RunOnsen
💍 Honeymoon tip — when booking, mention it's your honeymoon. Many ryokan will arrange a small gift (mochi, sake, flower arrangement) in the room, and some will upgrade your kashikiri bath time to a longer slot without charge. It costs nothing to ask.
⚠️ Book Ginzan ryokan well in advance — only ~10 properties in the whole village. Summer fills up months ahead. Notoya and Fujiya are the most-recommended.
